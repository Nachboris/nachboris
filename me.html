A bit about me

Hi, I'm Boris.

31, French, based in Lausanne with my wife Élodie — she teaches kids in a primary school here, the real hard job in the household. I do the laptop one. Voilà.

This page is the unfiltered version of me — the rabbit holes, the values, the dumb jokes. The CV-friendly stuff lives on /projects.

The short version

Born online, still here.

At 15, I sold $800 worth of in-game currency to a stranger on the internet. Hands shaking, brand new PayPal account, Guild Wars gold built up over thousands of hours of farming. A few weeks later, I found out the buyer had resold it for double. I wasn't annoyed for long — I'd just discovered there was a whole value chain behind a video game, and I was sitting at the bottom of it. Full story here if you're curious.

That trade was 16 years ago. Since then I've explored every corner of the field — botting, RMT, diversifying services — then professionalised the whole thing: built suppliers, an email list, a Discord community, went full-time, lived a year across Asia as a digital nomad, and somewhere along the way became the guy who listens to business and personal-finance podcasts no one around me wants to hear about. (Sorry, Élodie.)

Today I help solo founders and creators run the operational half of their business. I'm the wrong fit for big teams, equity-based deals, or anyone who needs a 12-month strategy deck — the right fit if you want a quiet pair of hands taking the unglamorous half off your plate.

What I actually value

Three things I won't compromise on.

If you took these three away, I'd have to find a different job.

01
Honesty — even when it's awkward.

I'll tell you on day three that something isn't working, instead of pretending on day thirty. If your idea is shaky, I'll say so — once, with tact — then it's your call. I'd rather lose a contract than write you a bill for three months of polite nodding.

02
Curiosity — the incurable kind.

I read on AI, business, finance and individual freedom. Probably more than I should. The upside for you: I bring context from outside your bubble — without pretending to be your strategist. I just connect dots that nobody around you has the bandwidth to connect.

03
Open-mindedness — default state.

Different industries, different personalities, different countries (I've lived in five). I genuinely don't care where the good idea comes from — the intern, the customer, the competitor, a Discord lurker at 2am. If it's true, it's true. Pride is the most expensive blocker in operations.
