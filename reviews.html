A confession

The day I asked for four stars.

For more than a decade, every single review I got was 5 stars. At some point a buyer pulled me aside and told me it looked too clean — almost suspicious.

So I asked the next happy customer to leave 4 stars instead of 5. He compromised. Called me "not the cheapest" — and meant it as a fact, not a complaint.

Turns out a small flaw is the most credible thing you can show.
