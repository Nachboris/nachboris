Act 1 — The side hustle

How an $800 trade turned into a 9-year side business

Between 2012 and 2021, Bobu ran in the background of my life — alongside high school, a Master in Digital Business Management at Rennes School of Business, and my early career in startups as a business developer.

I was reselling 8,000+ game keys and 100+ Collector's Editions across a decade. The real skill wasn't selling — it was finding supply nobody else had: retail clearance deals, marketplace arbitrage on Amazon, eBay and LeBonCoin, key-store pricing errors. Boring, patient work that compounded.

Reputation compounds faster than revenue. Every happy customer becomes a returning customer. Every returning customer brings referrals.

By the end of that period I was a known reference in my niche, with 1,000+ verified positive reviews, a 100% satisfaction rate, and a business running in parallel with a full-time job.

Act 2 — The ban wave

150 accounts, $15,000 lost, and the lesson that changed everything

At some point I did what most operators in this space eventually do: I tried to scale production. 150 accounts running in parallel. Bots generating supply. More output, thinner margins, endless maintenance. A twice-a-day routine, every day, no weekends.

Then came the ban wave. 150 accounts flagged in 48 hours — including 125 that weren't even botting. Estimated loss: $15,000 in inventory and accounts, gone in a weekend.

The ban wasn't the real problem. The ban was the moment I finally saw what I'd actually built: a production machine with a single point of failure, no customer list, no safety net. The fix was embarrassingly simple, and it took me years and $15K to see it.

I stopped trying to be the biggest producer and started being the most trusted seller. Everything changed.
Act 3 — Full-time, on the road

From side project to $500K+ business — while traveling Southeast Asia

In 2021, my girlfriend and I left for a year across Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam, Indonesia, South Korea, Malaysia and New Zealand. On paper, terrible timing to scale a business. In practice, the best thing that ever happened to it.

Living on the road forced decisions I'd been avoiding: every task that couldn't be automated, delegated, or systemized had to disappear or force a fix. Three levers did the heavy lifting — building a real community (a Discord server that became a trust machine), entering a bigger market with proper operational security, and using the low cost of living as runway to build patiently instead of grabbing every quick sale.

There was no dramatic "I quit my job" moment. By the end of that year, the business was doing better than a job. So I just didn't go back. For the next three years, this was my full-time income — properly declared, taxed, and profitable enough to fund the life that eventually let me buy real estate and move to Switzerland.

I shut it down in 2024, on my terms, with 2,250+ verified positive reviews and a 100% satisfaction rate across its entire history.
