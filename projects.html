Act 1 — The side hustle (2012 — 2021)

Nine years of services for serious gamers

It started at 15, selling $800 of in-game currency to a stranger on the internet. My hands were shaking when I clicked confirm. That single trade opened the door to nine years of side-hustling.

Over time, the offer kept widening. It went from "I can sell you currency" to a full menu of services for serious players: in-game items, escrow (secured trades between strangers), premade accounts, and activation keys — from mainstream releases to rarer items like Collector's Editions and pre-orders. At first, everything was done by hand: every key delivered one at a time, every trade walked through message by message. Over the years, I gradually automated the parts that could be — starting with the highest-volume ones — while keeping the human touch where it actually mattered to the customer.

Reputation compounds faster than revenue. Every happy customer becomes a returning customer. Every returning customer brings referrals.

By the end of that period I was a known reference in my niche, with 1,000+ verified positive reviews, a 100% satisfaction rate, and a business that ran in parallel with a full-time job — without a single dispute I couldn't close cleanly.

Act 2 — From producer to reseller

The shift that turned the operation into a real business

Early on, I did what most operators in this space eventually do: I tried to scale by becoming the producer. Bots generating supply, accounts running in parallel, the whole setup — what looked like "passive income" on paper. I ran it that way for years.

It took me a long time to admit it wasn't actually passive. It was a treadmill: more output, thinner margins, endless maintenance, and a single point of failure I'd never really own. The shift in thinking was slow, and honestly, humbling.

So I made the opposite move. I stopped trying to be the biggest producer and started being the most trusted seller. I built a network of reliable suppliers, sourced inventory through them, and put my energy into the storefront, the customer relationship, and the diversification of services.

That's when the offer really matured: premade accounts sold to players, a middleman service to secure high-value trades between strangers, and operational standards that other sellers simply didn't have. The middleman work in particular — sitting between two strangers making a risky exchange — was the seed of an idea I'd later turn into my marketplace project.

I stopped trying to be the biggest producer and started being the most trusted seller. Everything changed.
Act 3 — Full-time, on the road (2021 — 2024)

From side project to $500K+ business — while traveling Southeast Asia

In 2021, my girlfriend and I left for a year across Thailand, Cambodia, Vietnam, Indonesia, South Korea, Malaysia and New Zealand. On paper, terrible timing to scale a business. In practice, the best thing that ever happened to it.

Living on the road forced decisions I'd been avoiding: every task that couldn't be automated had to disappear or force a fix. I built a custom WordPress storefront (GW2Rewards), fully automated CD-key delivery end-to-end, ran email campaigns through MailerLite, and shifted into a bigger market with proper operational security. The low cost of living gave me runway to build patiently instead of grabbing every quick sale.

There was no dramatic "I quit my job" moment. By the end of that year, the business was doing better than a job. So I just didn't go back. For the next three years, this was my full-time income — properly declared, taxed, and profitable enough to generate $500K+ in cumulative revenue, fund the life I wanted, and invest in real estate back in France.

By 2024, with 2,250+ verified positive reviews and a 100% satisfaction rate across its entire history, I'd stretched that model about as far as it could go alone. I closed it on my terms — to go build something more ambitious: a proper marketplace.

Gamoniz (ex-Marketplace)

Active
Late 2024 — present

Originally launched as a digital-goods marketplace backed by a crowdfunding campaign that raised 226% of its goal, with a small team of 3 interns I managed through brand, go-to-market, WordPress build and launch.
